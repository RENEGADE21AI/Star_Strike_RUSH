# Star Strike RUSH — Game Design Document

### Living Design Reference

-----

## 1. Vision & Concept

Star Strike RUSH is a single-screen vertical shoot-’em-up (shmup) designed for portrait mobile play, playable in a browser with no install. It targets the feel of classic arcade shmups — tight, readable action, escalating pressure, short-but-intense sessions — while layering in a small number of original mechanics (the realm system, fractional damage, boss alternation) that give it a distinct identity without overwhelming complexity.

The tone is dark and cinematic but not grimdark. The palette skews deep space: near-black backgrounds, purple-black fog, vivid enemy colors that pop against the darkness. The game should feel tense and fast, with a slight weight to the player ship that rewards aggressive positioning rather than timid corner-camping.

The intended play session is 5–15 minutes. Death should feel fair. Every loss should feel survivable-in-retrospect, motivating a retry.

-----

## 2. Platform & Presentation

**Logical resolution:** 375 × 667 px (9:16 portrait)  
**Rendering:** HTML5 Canvas, single self-contained HTML file  
**Scaling:** Letterboxed — scales uniformly to fill any device screen. Fog bands fill the letterbox bars so there are never raw black bars. The game area is always centered.  
**Input:** Touch (primary) and mouse. All pointer coordinates are transformed through the offset/scale matrix so game logic is resolution-independent.  
**Target device:** Mobile browser (portrait). Also playable on desktop.

There is no install, no account, no server. All persistence is localStorage.

-----

## 3. Core Gameplay Loop

The player pilots a single ship from the bottom of the screen, shooting upward at descending enemy waves. Enemies shoot back. The player can move freely across the full game area and has a ghost-dash ability for evasion.

Waves of enemies arrive in formation. Between waves, the game briefly pauses before the next set spawns. Bosses appear at fixed phase milestones. The loop is:

**Wave → clear → brief pause → next wave → … → Boss phase → repeat at higher difficulty**

There are no lives in the traditional sense — the player has a hit-point total, and the game ends when it reaches zero. Powerups dropped by enemies restore HP, upgrade firepower, or add a wingman escort.

Scoring is cumulative. There is a single global high score (localStorage). No online leaderboard. The player sets a call sign that appears on the game over and high score screen.

-----

## 4. Player Ship

### Movement

Free movement across the full game area. Touch/drag to reposition. The ship follows the input point with slight physics smoothing — responsive but not teleporting. There is intentional inertia so the ship feels like it has mass.

### Firing

The ship fires automatically at all times while alive. Shot type depends on current powerup state and whether the Wraith fight is active.

**Shot types:**

- **Standard single:** One center bullet, full damage (1.0)
- **Spread:** Three bullets (left, center, right). Side bullets deal fractional damage (0.75). Center deals full (1.0).
- **Wingman:** Escort ship fires independently. Wingman side shots also deal 0.75 fractional damage.

The fractional damage system prevents spread fire from being a strict upgrade — it deals more total damage against a wide group but less per-hit against a single armored target like a boss.

### Ghost Dash

The player can activate a brief ghost state (the “dash”). While ghosted:

- The ship becomes semi-transparent with a soft outer glow
- Scan lines are drawn over the ship geometry
- Six small motes orbit the ship in a loose ring
- The ship is immune to physical-realm enemy bullets
- The ship can pass through physical enemies without collision

The ghost dash has a cooldown. It is not a movement teleport — the ship does not reposition. It is purely a brief invulnerability/phase-through window.

**Visual style of ghost:** Transparent hull (~46% alpha) + outer aura rendered with a lighter composite mode + scan line overlay + 6 orbiting motes. No afterimage trails. The ghost animation design may be revised in a future pass.

### Health

The player has a hit point total displayed as a bar in the HUD. At low HP, a pulsing red border warning activates around the game area. There is also a screen flash on taking damage.

No shield. No extra lives. One ship, one HP pool.

-----

## 5. Wingman

A powerup can grant the player a wingman escort ship. The wingman:

- Orbits or flanks the player ship
- Fires independently, matching the player’s shot rhythm
- Has its own HP or is destroyed on a single boss-level hit (TBD)
- Visually distinct from the player ship but in the same design language

The wingman adds firepower and serves as a soft “extra hit” since certain enemy bullets may target the player position and be intercepted.

-----

## 6. Progression — Phases, Waves, Difficulty

### Phases

The game is divided into phases. Phases gate boss encounters and difficulty scaling. Phase number is the primary progression counter.

- Phases 1–7: Standard bosses appear at every phase divisible by 4 (phase 4 only in this range)
- Phase 8+: Boss encounters alternate between Standard and Wraith bosses

This means the Wraith is introduced as a late-game escalation, not a recurring threat from the start.

### Waves

Each phase contains multiple enemy waves. Waves are drawn from a library of wave templates that define enemy count, type, formation pattern, and entry choreography. Wave templates are never modified by difficulty scaling code — the templates are fixed design assets.

**Wave mood** is a meta-parameter that influences which templates are selected and how aggressively enemies fire. Mood shifts over the course of a phase.

### Difficulty Scaling

A difficulty value increases over time and phase count. It affects:

- Enemy movement speed
- Enemy bullet speed and fire rate
- Frequency of elite (purple/phantom) enemies in formations

Scaling uses the `updateDifficulty()` and `updatePressure()` systems. Pressure is a shorter-term spike that temporarily increases aggression during boss approach or after the player has been doing very well.

-----

## 7. Enemy Roster

All enemies are rendered geometrically — no sprites. Each has a distinct silhouette, color, and behavior pattern.

### Red — Scout

- HP: 2
- Threat contribution: 1.05
- The standard footsoldier. Fast, low HP. Often appears in large formations. Dies in two hits from a center shot or a couple of spread hits. Fires occasionally.

### Orange — Light

- HP: 1
- Threat contribution: 0.80
- Frailer than red, often used as filler or chaff in mixed formations. One hit to kill. May appear in larger numbers to compensate.

### Purple — Heavy

- HP: 5
- Threat contribution: 3.55
- The armored threat. Takes sustained fire to kill. Moves slower. Fires more dangerous bullet patterns. Appears in smaller numbers but dramatically raises the difficulty of a wave.

### Phantom

- HP: 3
- Threat contribution: 2.35
- The special enemy. Alternates between physical and ghost phases on a fixed cycle (~132 frames physical, ~78 frames ghost). During its ghost phase, it is immune to physical-realm player bullets. During the physical phase, it is fully vulnerable.
- Has a 20-frame telegraph (flicker) before each phase transition so the player can anticipate it.
- The Phantom fires “phantom shots” — cross-realm bullets (realm: null) that can hit the player regardless of ghost dash state. This makes the Phantom uniquely dangerous and not safely ignored during a dash.

**Enemy visual language:** All enemies have triangular engine flame indicators (not round or rectangular nozzles). Color-coding is consistent — enemy type is always identifiable at a glance by hue.

-----

## 8. Boss Encounters

### Standard Boss

A large, heavily armored ship that drops in from the top of the screen. Fires complex bullet patterns in phases. The spawn attack has no telegraph — the pause before it fires is the only warning. This is intentional; the timing is sufficient and a visual telegraph was judged as redundant and visually noisy.

The Standard Boss cycles through attack patterns as its HP depletes. It is always targetable with physical-realm bullets.

### Wraith Boss

The Wraith is the late-game signature encounter. It operates across two realms:

- In its physical phase, it is vulnerable to physical bullets
- In its ghost phase, it is only vulnerable to ghost-realm bullets (fired while the player is in ghost state)

The Wraith fires bullets gated by realm — its physical bullets can only hit a physical-realm player, its ghost bullets can only hit a ghost-realm player. This creates a dynamic where the player must actively manage realm state both to deal damage and to survive.

The Wraith has a distinct visual treatment from the Standard Boss — ghostly, translucent, with aura effects matching the phantom enemy aesthetic.

### Boss Phase Rotation

- Phases 1–7: Standard boss appears only at phase milestones (phase % 4 == 0)
- Phase 8+: Bosses alternate — Standard, Wraith, Standard, Wraith…

This ensures the player encounters the Wraith only after they’ve had time to understand the realm mechanic through Phantom enemies.

-----

## 9. The Realm System

The realm system is the game’s central original mechanic, fully realized during the Wraith fight.

**Player realm:** 0 = physical, 1 = ghost (activated by ghost dash)  
**Bullet realm:** Each bullet carries the realm of the player at the moment of firing  
**Collision gating:** A bullet only damages a target if the bullet’s realm matches the target’s current realm (or the bullet is cross-realm, realm: null)

**Phantom shot** (fired by Phantom enemies): realm: null — hits regardless of player state. This is intentional and permanent design. It is the Phantom’s defining threat.

**Player ghost dash:** Fires physical bullets at all times unless explicitly in ghost state. Managing the timing of ghost dash to fire ghost bullets at the Wraith’s ghost phase while also surviving Wraith ghost bullets is the core skill challenge of the Wraith fight.

The realm system does not affect normal (non-boss) gameplay in a gameplay-blocking way — it is mostly background during regular waves. Phantoms introduce it visually and mechanically before the Wraith makes it load-bearing.

-----

## 10. Bullet System

### Bullet Budget

To prevent bullet spam from overwhelming the screen, a bullet budget system caps total active bullets:

- Regular combat: max 36 active enemy bullets
- Wraith fight: max 54 active enemy bullets

Before any enemy fires, `canSpendBulletBudget()` is checked. If the budget is spent, the fire is skipped. This prevents frame rate degradation and visual noise on weaker devices.

### Fractional Damage

Not all bullets deal the same damage. Spread fire side bullets and wingman side shots deal 0.75 damage. Center shots deal 1.0. Boss bullets deal higher values. This is stored as `b.damage ?? 1` in collision — the fallback ensures legacy bullets always deal 1.

This system means players can’t mindlessly spray and expect full DPS against armored targets. Precise aiming matters against high-HP enemies.

-----

## 11. Powerups

Powerups drop from destroyed enemies (chance-based). Types include:

- **HP restore** — partial heal
- **Spread upgrade** — grants spread shot
- **Wingman** — grants escort ship
- (Possibly others — additional types may be added in future passes)

### Drought Prevention

If the player goes 12 kills or 900 frames without a powerup drop, a drop is guaranteed. This prevents frustrating dry streaks where the player is weakening through sustained combat with no recovery.

-----

## 12. Scoring & Persistence

**Score:** Accumulated by killing enemies. Enemy type and threat value influence score contribution. Boss kills give large score bonuses.

**High score:** Stored in localStorage (`star_strike_rush_high_score_v1`). Compared against on game over. The previous high score is captured before the new score is committed, so the game over screen can show both the old record and the new result.

**Call sign:** Player-entered name/tag stored in localStorage (`star_strike_rush_callsign_v1`). Displayed on game over and high score screens.

**Settings persistence:** Max particle count and screen shake preference stored in localStorage (`star_strike_rush_settings_v1`).

**Codex:** A discovered-enemy log stored in localStorage (`star_strike_rush_codex_v1`). Tracks which enemy types the player has encountered. Intended as a light meta-progression reward for curious players — no gameplay gating.

-----

## 13. Visual Design

### Overall Aesthetic

Dark space. Deep blacks and near-blacks for the background. Enemy ships use vivid saturated colors (red, orange, purple) that read instantly against the background. The player ship is lighter, distinct. The UI uses minimal chrome — HP bar, score text, phase indicator. Nothing decorative that competes with the action.

The feel should be slightly brutal and intense — not cute, not retro-pixel, but geometric and precise. Think vector arcade updated for mobile.

### Color Palette (Fog / Letterbox)

The fog bands that fill the letterbox areas (outside the 375×667 game area) use a dark purple-black palette. RGB values roughly in the ranges: R 12–32, G 5–14, B 26–58. This connects the game area to the device frame rather than having raw black bars.

The game area itself is punched to near-black (#040208) after fog is drawn — not pure black, slightly purple-tinted to keep the palette coherent.

### Fog System (Confirmed, Locked Design)

Three-tier cloud system rendered in the letterbox bands:

1. **Large masses** — 4 overlapping lobes plus a dense core
1. **Mid rolling structures** — 2 lobes, denser than wisps
1. **Faint wisps** — the lightest layer, adding depth

Each fog element has multi-oscillator drift: 3 independent sine/cosine terms at different frequencies per axis, producing organic non-repeating movement.

The fog is clipped to the letterbox bands (outside game area). Seam feathering bleeds slightly inward across the boundary for a soft transition. The game area is punched black after fog draw. The fog fill always renders before any early-exit check, preventing the black-screen bug where skipping early could leave the canvas unfilled.

### Title Screen

- Animated fog bands as described above
- Two suns visible in the game area background (intentional, not a bug)
- Enemy ships drift across the title in formation, sinking into the fog at the edges (controlled by a smoothstep falloff over ~96px rather than a hard clip)
- Game title and start prompt overlaid

### Enemy Visuals

- All engines/exhausts rendered as triangles
- Player exhaust has no dark nozzle block
- Each enemy type has a distinct silhouette readable at small sizes

### Ghost / Phantom Visual

Currently: semi-transparent hull (~46% alpha), outer aura via lighter composite, scan line overlay, 6 orbiting motes.
**Status: Under review. The ghost animation may be redesigned in a future pass.**

### Particle System

Explosions, bullet impacts, and damage events use a particle system. Max particle count is a user-configurable setting (for lower-end devices). Screen shake on impacts is also configurable.

## 16. Screen Shake & Juice

Screen shake is applied to the game area transform (not the fog layer, which is fixed). Shake intensity scales with event severity:

- Minor: bullet hit on player
- Major: boss death, player death

The fog and letterbox remain stable during shake to avoid disorienting the player about the screen boundary.

## 17. Locked Design Decisions

These are confirmed and must not be revisited without explicit intent:

|Decision                                                     |Rationale                              |
|-------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
|Double sun on title screen                                   |Intentional aesthetic, not a bug       |
|Standard boss spawn attack has no telegraph                  |Timing pause is sufficient warning     |
|Ghost dash: transparent + glowy, NO afterimage trails        |Afterimage looked cluttered            |
|Enemy engines are triangles                                  |Consistent visual language             |
|Player exhaust has no dark nozzle block                      |Cleaner silhouette                     |
|Phantom shot is cross-realm (realm: null)                    |Core threat identity of the Phantom    |
|Powerup drought: 12 kills or 900 frames guaranteed           |Player experience, prevents frustration|
|Previous high score captured before update in enterGameOver()|Allows accurate before/after display   |
|Bullet budget: 36 normal / 54 wraith                         |Frame rate and visual clarity          |

## 18. Architecture Notes (For Context Continuity)

These are implementation anchors — not game design, but important for preserving design intent across chat sessions:

- `STORAGE_KEY = "star_strike_rush_high_score_v1"` — never change
- `updateCollisions()` — entire function is do-not-touch
- `fireEnemyBullet()`, `fireWraithBullet()` — entire functions preserved
- All realm logic: `playerRealm`, `isWraithActive()`, `getPlayerShotKind()`
- `updateBossStandard()`, `updateBossWraith()` — do not rewrite
- All wave templates and `waveTemplates` object — design assets, not code
- `b.damage ?? 1` in collision — the fallback is load-bearing
- `phantomShot` fires with `realm: null` — cross-realm by design

**draw() render order (critical):**

1. `drawOuterFog()` — first, clears canvas, fills fog bands, punches game area black
1. `ctx.save()` → translate → scale → translate(shake)
1. `drawBackground()`
1. Game content by state
1. `ctx.restore()`
1. If playing/game over: save → translate → scale → `drawLowHpWarning` → `drawDamageFlash` → restore
