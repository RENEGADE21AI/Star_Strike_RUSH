Star Strike RUSH Design Blueprint

Arcade clarity before feature sprawl

This document is the visual contract for the game screens. It keeps the product portrait-first, readable under motion, and arcade-shmup focused. Long meta surfaces are drawn as stretched screens so layout decisions are visible without needing to imagine scroll.

ViewportBase design is 375 x 667 portrait. Desktop centers the same playfield.
MaterialDark glass panels, thin light strokes, neon status colors, hard-edged arcade geometry.
TypographyImpact-style display for drama, Arial Narrow UI for dense labels and score readouts.
MotionReserved for combat, warnings, reward pulses, and panel transitions. No busy idle UI.

Play First

Every non-combat surface points back to Play. The title screen is not a dashboard.

One Job Per Screen

Account owns profile, sync, and settings. Records, achievements, progress, and codex stay focused.

Fair Readability

Combat UI must leave center and lower-middle playfield open for dodging.

No Cosmetic Economy

Progression rewards are Glory, Credits, Season XP, records, and achievements. Do not add dress-up economies to this game.

Current

Title Screen

The first screen is a playable arcade cockpit, not a landing page. It should say: choose a pilot identity, press Play, or inspect one focused meta surface.

  • Logo sits in the upper third, readable over menu-flight background.
  • Currency values sit at the top: Glory, Credits, and current Season Tier.
  • Call sign is the pilot-card anchor; account chip sits to the left and opens profile/settings.
  • Play is the dominant action and wider than every other control.
  • Icon order: Badges, Road, Records, Codex.
  • The icon row should read as a small Pilot Meta dock so players understand these are secondary progression/profile surfaces.
  • The account chip should be labelled as Account so sync/profile is not confused with the call sign field.
  • High score and Glory rank stay below the icon row as a compact status strip.
Anti-pattern: do not add a large dashboard, news carousel, or monetization callout to this screen.

Account Panel

Account is the pilot utility screen. It tells the player whether online sync is available and gives safe controls for sign-in, sign-out, sync, settings, and reset.

  • Opens from the small account chip, not the main icon row.
  • Status and errors live above buttons and never overlap actions.
  • Shows local rank/Glory/Tier/Credits even when signed out.
  • Settings live here: particle count, screen shake, and reset progress.
  • Refresh remains available but should not feel like a required pre-run step.

Badges / Achievements

This is a trophy case. It should be satisfying at a glance and useful when long: unlocked state, locked silhouette, requirement, and recent unlocks.

  • Header summary: unlocked count plus progress bar.
  • Rows stretch vertically when achievements exceed the viewport.
  • Locked rows should still communicate goals, not hide everything behind mystery.
  • Online sync message stays at the bottom of the full long surface.

Glory Road

Glory Road is lifetime identity. It should feel like a classic scrollable progression road: a long rail, big rank milestones, smaller checkpoints, and visible distance to the next prestige moment.

  • Top tabs switch Glory Road and Season Road.
  • Current rank is the headline, with next rank and progress bar pinned near the top.
  • The road itself is long, with alternating rank cards on a central rail.
  • Major ranks get larger glowing nodes; minor checkpoints preview the climb between ranks.
  • Opening the Road should auto-position near the player's current rank, with a little space above it.
  • The current node should have extra energy: pulse, glow, and a small ship marker on the rail.
  • Tapping a node opens an inspect panel with requirement, status, and identity reward.
  • Use gold for lifetime identity, green for reached status.

Season Road

Season Road is the temporary 8-week progress surface. It should feel like a classic long progression road: lots of tiers, a central spine, frequent small resource rewards, and bigger milestone rewards every few steps.

  • Use green/cyan instead of gold so it does not compete with lifetime Glory.
  • Tier rows become a long scroll track, not a compact list.
  • Flight rewards sit on the left lane; Supply rewards sit on the right lane. Both lanes are earned through play.
  • Reward cards use real reward data: tier, reward type, amount/name, detail text, and claimed/unclaimed/locked state.
  • Opening the Season Road should auto-position near the player's current tier.
  • Every fifth tier should feel like a larger milestone node.
  • Reward claiming happens here through the selected reward detail panel, not directly on the title screen.

World Records

The records screen answers one question: how do I compare? It should be fast to scan and not mixed with account controls.

  • Signed-out state tells player to use Account, not a duplicate login flow.
  • Leaderboard rows show place, call sign, rank label, and best score.
  • Player personal best can pin above or below the global list later.
  • Refresh button stays fixed at bottom of the panel.

Codex Grid

The codex is discovery and threat literacy. It should help players recognize silhouettes, colors, and mechanics.

  • Cards are compact, color-coded, and use ship silhouettes before discovery.
  • Use larger 3-column specimen cards when the roster is expanded so fighter shape is readable on mobile.
  • Enemy marks are shorthand only; final art should read as real fighter craft with role-specific hardware.
  • Grid can stretch as enemies and bosses grow; do not squeeze below readability.
  • New discoveries badge the Codex icon on the title screen.
  • Tap a discovered card to open detail.

Codex Detail

Detail pages should explain one threat without becoming a manual. The goal is recognition, not encyclopedic lore.

  • Back control is top-left inside the card.
  • Use a wide specimen bay for the ship art before stats so new enemy silhouettes can be inspected.
  • Enemy art is large enough to inspect silhouette, hull language, color, and role cue.
  • Threat, HP, speed, aggression, and fire rate are the compact stat set.
  • Later: add mechanic tip, first-seen phase, and best counter.

Reset Confirm

This modal must interrupt gently but clearly. It is destructive and should not be easy to tap by accident.

  • Dim all background content.
  • Show player call sign in the confirmation body.
  • No and Yes sit apart; Yes is red.
  • Future: require hold-to-confirm if remote account data is affected.

Gameplay HUD

The playfield is the product. HUD elements are edge-bound and low-chrome so the player can read bullets and movement threats.

  • Top-left: HP segments and energy bar.
  • Top-right: score, high score, combo multiplier.
  • Bottom-left: virtual joystick.
  • Bottom-right: Ghost action button.
  • Center/lower-middle stays clear for ship, bullets, enemies, and powerups.

Boss / Warning HUD

Boss patterns need special priority. The boss bar shifts the HUD down and warnings must be visible before danger.

  • Boss HP sits top center.
  • Player HUD offsets down during boss fights.
  • Warning text appears in a compact center chip.
  • Rail/laser warnings use red charge lines that lock before firing.
  • Debris fields use obvious text before hazards spawn.

Wraith Realm

Wraith mode changes the action button from Ghost Dash to realm hop. The screen tint can help, but it must never hide bullets or ship state.

  • Action button label becomes Hop.
  • Realm state is reflected in button color and boss/player effects.
  • Physical and ghost threats stay readable by color, not just opacity.
  • Energy cost is lower during realm hopping.

Game Over

Game Over is the reward and retry surface. It should show the run result, new records, meta gains, and get the player back into another run quickly.

  • Game Over title sits top third.
  • Score and high score appear before meta gains.
  • Glory/Season/Credits preview sits above buttons.
  • Respawn is primary; View Road is the meta bridge; Title Screen is secondary.
  • Future: new achievements should appear as compact reward chips here.

Future Meta Map

These are not implemented screens yet. They are included so future progression and utility work has guardrails before code starts.

  • Missions: daily and weekly objectives that reward Season XP, Glory, or Credits.
  • Practice: a low-pressure training surface for dash timing, boss warnings, and enemy study.
  • Accessibility: audio, contrast, input, and readable-warning controls.
  • Run History: recent local and synced runs with score, phase, rank, and road gains.
