# Star Strike RUSH - Game Design Document

## Living Design Reference

This document is the source of truth for Star Strike RUSH. Keep it updated whenever gameplay, progression, enemies, bosses, powerups, online systems, or major technical structure changes.

## 1. Vision

Star Strike RUSH is a single-screen vertical shmup built for portrait mobile play. It should feel like a fast arcade run: short sessions, readable threats, escalating pressure, fair deaths, and immediate restarts.

The game identity is:

- Tight single-screen vertical action.
- Geometric enemy silhouettes with strong color identity.
- Fair telegraphs instead of surprise deaths.
- Ghost dash and energy management as the signature player resource.
- Short waves that create pressure without flooding the screen.
- Bosses that test one clear skill at a time.

Do not add more enemies, bosses, or powerups beyond the roster in this document until the current package has been tuned, balanced, and polished.

## 2. Platform And Structure

Logical resolution: 375 x 667 portrait.

Rendering: HTML5 Canvas.

Input: mobile touch controls first, keyboard/mouse also supported.

Live Firebase Hosting URL: `https://star-strike-rush.web.app`.

Persistence:

- Local high score, call sign, settings, and codex discoveries use localStorage.
- Local Glory, current-season XP preview, Credits preview, lifetime stats, and
  recent run receipts use localStorage.
- Firebase Auth supports Google sign-in.
- Cloud Firestore stores online profiles, player-card progression fields, world
  record leaderboard entries, immutable run receipts, and earned achievements.
- Cloud Functions source exists for server-side run receipt validation and
  Season reward claiming. Deployment requires the Firebase project to be on the
  Blaze plan.
- The game remains playable signed out; online sync is additive.

Source layout:

- `index.html` loads ordered browser scripts.
- `styles.css` owns page/canvas styling.
- `docs/design-blueprint.html` is the repo-native screen design blueprint. It
  shows current screens, long stretched layouts for scrollable panels, and
  future meta-screen guardrails. Update it before making major UI changes.
- `src/01-core.js` through `src/18-session-input-loop.js` own the core game.
- `src/12-rendering-progress-road.js` owns the title-screen Glory Road and
  Season Road renderer, season reward definitions, Road hit testing, and
  current-progress focus behavior.
- `src/18-expansion-data.js`, `src/18-expansion-enemies-powerups.js`,
  `src/18-expansion-hazards-bosses.js`, and
  `src/18-expansion-rendering-waves.js` own the final expansion package.
- `src/19-game-achievements.js` defines achievement, run receipt, and online payloads.
- `src/20-firebase-online.js` owns Firebase Auth and Firestore sync.
- `functions/` owns callable Firebase Functions and shared server progression
  math for authoritative online rewards.

## 3. Core Loop

The player pilots a ship near the bottom of the screen and fires automatically upward.

The loop is:

Wave -> brief pressure shift -> next wave -> phase clear -> boss milestone -> higher phase.

The player has HP and energy. HP loss ends the run at zero. Energy fuels ghost dash and becomes more important once Siphons, Leeches, Siphon Core, Stabilizer, and Overcharge enter the game.

Scoring is cumulative. Combo kills increase the multiplier. Score Surge temporarily boosts score gain.

At the end of a run, Score converts to Glory at 10:1:

Glory gained = floor(score / 10).

Glory is permanent lifetime identity progression. It never decreases and must
never unlock HP, damage, better ghost dash, better powerup odds, boss advantages,
or gameplay score multipliers.

## 4. Player Ship

### Movement

The player moves freely inside the play area with smoothed acceleration. The ship should feel responsive but not weightless. Mobile controls must remain readable and forgiving.

### Firing

The ship fires automatically.

Shot types:

- Standard single: one center shot, 1.0 damage.
- Spread Shot: three shots, center at 1.0 damage and side shots at 0.75 damage.
- Wingman: escort ships fire independently.
- Piercing Shot: center shot pierces through one enemy for a short duration.

### Ghost Dash And Energy

Ghost dash grants a brief phase window and costs energy. During Wraith fights, ghost action switches the player between physical and ghost realms.

Energy regenerates over time. Overcharge increases regeneration temporarily. Energy Cell restores a chunk immediately. Siphon and Leech threats attack this resource without turning empty energy into heavy HP damage.

### Health And Defense

The player has one HP pool. Repair +1 restores HP up to max.

Phase Shield blocks one HP damage instance, flashes, and breaks. It does not block energy drain.

## 5. Meta Layer, Online Records, And Achievements

The meta layer supports the arcade loop without taking it over. The title screen
still prioritizes Play, with compact meta entry points placed around the main
start flow instead of a dashboard-style menu. Progression is mostly presented
after the run, but the title screen should always make long-term identity and
goals visible.

Current implemented meta layer:

- Score converts to Glory at 10:1.
- Glory ranks appear on the title screen, game-over screen, online player card,
  and leaderboard rows.
- End-of-run progression shows Glory gained, total Glory, rank, Season XP gained,
  Credits earned, and current season tier.
- Run receipts record score, phase reached, duration, enemies killed, bosses
  killed, powerups collected, ghost uses, damage taken, highest combo, and client
  version.
- Local guest progression works signed out. Signed-in accounts sync public
  profile fields, leaderboard records, achievements, and owner-only receipt
  archives.
- Season Road rewards are claimable. Claimed state persists, rewards apply to
  Credits, Glory, or Season XP, and signed-out players use local fallback.
- Signed-in run submissions are wired to `submitRunReceipt()` so the server can
  validate plausibility and compute Glory, Season XP, Credits, achievements, and
  leaderboard records after Functions are deployed.

Glory ranks:

- 0: Rookie Pilot.
- 1,000: Star Cadet.
- 3,000: Strike Pilot.
- 7,500: Void Runner.
- 15,000: Ace.
- 30,000: Elite Ace.
- 60,000: Phantom Hunter.
- 100,000: Wraithbreaker.
- 175,000: Solar Legend.
- 300,000: Star Eternal.

Season structure target:

- 8-week seasons.
- 50 Season Road tiers.
- Flight reward lane for Season XP and Glory caches.
- Supply reward lane for Credits.
- Daily and weekly missions later.
- No season gameplay events.

Meta systems that are planned but not implemented yet:

- Daily missions.
- Weekly missions.
- Practice/training screen.
- Run history screen.
- Accessibility/audio controls.
- AdMob rewarded ads.

Hard monetization and community constraints:

- No fan-fund or fuel system.
- No loot boxes.
- No paid gameplay advantage.
- No permanent gameplay upgrades.
- No gameplay power from purchases, Glory, Credits, or seasons.

Stripe is not added yet. If web payments are ever added, Stripe must be handled
by server-side checkout and webhooks, and clients must not grant purchases or
spendable balances directly. Android/iOS builds should use a purchase-provider
abstraction for platform billing.

Signed-in players can sync:

- Profile call sign.
- Best score.
- Player-card progression fields.
- Run receipts.
- Earned achievements.
- Global leaderboard records.

Title screen meta placement:

- Play remains the largest central action.
- Glory, Credits, and current Season Tier are visible at the top of the title
  screen and in title-side meta screens.
- The call sign area doubles as the pilot card, showing current Glory rank and
  total Glory.
- A small account chip sits to the left of the call sign. It opens account
  sign-in, sign-out, profile sync, online refresh, settings, and reset controls.
- The main icon row under Play is: Badges, Road, Records, Codex.
- Badges opens the achievements trophy panel.
- Road opens Glory Road and Season Road tabs. These tracks should feel like
  classic long-scroll progression roads with a central spine, nodes, milestone
  rewards, and visible travel distance. Do not compress them into short lists.
  The current canvas implementation uses a draggable/wheel-scroll road viewport:
  Glory alternates rank milestones and checkpoints, while Season displays all
  50 supported tiers on a central rail with Flight and Supply reward markers.
  Road opens around current progress instead of always starting at tier/rank 1.
  The current node has a pulsing marker ship, and tapping nodes or reward cards
  opens an inspect panel with requirement, reward, and reached/locked/claimed
  state. Unclaimed Season rewards can be claimed from the detail panel.
- Records opens the world-record leaderboard and refresh controls.
- Codex opens enemy and boss discovery.

The old combined Online drawer should not be the default information model.
Account, achievements, records, and progression should remain distinct so each
screen has one obvious purpose.

The old standalone settings screen should not return. Settings belong inside
Account because they are personal/profile utility, not a fifth title-screen
destination.

Game over should bridge into meta progression. After a run, the player sees
Glory, Season XP, Credits, rank/tier movement, and a `VIEW ROAD` action that
opens the Road panel near the newly relevant progress.

The game must remain functional if Firebase is unavailable or the player is signed out. In that case, local play and local high score continue normally.

Competitive production rule: Firestore rules can protect owner scope and data
shape, but they cannot prove a browser-reported run is real. The repo includes
`submitRunReceipt()` and `claimSeasonReward()` Cloud Functions for this, but the
Firebase project must be upgraded to Blaze before Functions can be deployed.
After deployment is verified, Firestore rules should be tightened so browser
clients no longer write leaderboard/progression grants directly.

## 6. Progression And Boss Rotation

Phases gate enemy introductions, debris events, and bosses.

Boss milestones occur on phases divisible by 4.

Current boss order:

- Phase 4: Standard Boss.
- Phase 8: Wraith Boss.
- Phase 12: Debris Warden.
- Phase 16: Mothership.
- Phase 20: Siphon Core.
- Phase 24: Hive Breaker.
- Phase 28: Rail Tyrant.
- Phase 32: Gravity Well.
- Later phase milestones cycle through the six expansion bosses.

The Wraith remains the first full realm-system boss. Expansion bosses come after the player has already seen the basic and realm mechanics.

## 7. Wave And Difficulty Rules

Waves are selected from templates and filtered by mood, phase, pressure, and active threat load.

Wave moods:

- Open: baseline readable patterns.
- Spike: sharper pressure and priority targets.
- Recovery: reduced pressure, safer drops, and less density.
- Rule: mechanic-focused waves such as Phantoms, Siphons, Leeches, support enemies, or Railgunners.

Difficulty scaling affects movement, firing cadence, enemy mix, pressure budget, and cooldowns. It must not override fairness rules:

- No unavoidable laser hits.
- No heavy bullet spam during asteroid walls.
- No Minecaster overload during debris fields.
- No debris event when the player is at 1 HP.
- No boss-shielding.
- No shield stacking.

Opening difficulty targets:

- First 30 seconds: readable warmup, phase 1, no bullet flood, mostly open or
  recovery wave moods.
- First 2 minutes: gentle ramp, no first boss yet, no normal bullet flood, and
  pressure should reflect actual screen danger rather than combo success alone.
- Mid-game: pressure can rise through spikes and rule waves, but enemy counts,
  bullets, pending spawns, and support enemies should stay within visible
  dodging space.
- Low HP: hits force a recovery window, stretch wave gaps, trim queued spawns,
  and reduce active bullet pressure outside boss-locked patterns.

Debug mode (`?debug`) records a bounded difficulty sample history containing
frame/time, phase, HP, energy, pressure, threat, bullet budget/load, enemy and
spawn counts, wave mood, intensity phase, relief state, kills, damage taken,
and deaths. Use this sampling when tuning opening, mid-game, and low-HP relief.

## 8. Enemy Roster

All enemies are geometric, color-coded, and readable at a glance.

### Red Scout

Color: red.
HP: 2.
Role: basic chaff and formation filler.

Drifts downward and establishes the baseline dodge/shoot rhythm.

### Orange Light Interceptor

Color: orange.
HP: 1.
Role: fast movement threat.

Uses erratic movement patterns and creates lane pressure without much durability.

### Purple Heavy

Color: purple.
HP: 5.
Role: armored shooter.

Slow, durable, and fires aimed shots after a visible warning ring.

### Cyan Phantom

Color: cyan.
HP: 3.
Role: realm enemy.

Alternates between physical and ghost states with a telegraph. During ghost state, it requires ghost-realm interaction in Wraith contexts.

### Splitter

Color: yellow-green shell with red/orange cracks.
Phase: 3+.
HP: 3.
Role: break-apart movement threat.

Does not shoot. When destroyed, it splits into two fragile shards that fly diagonally downward. Shards are worth little score and do not drop powerups.

### Carrier

Color: bronze/gold.
Phase: 6+.
HP: 6.
Role: priority spawner.

Slowly descends and opens launch bays. If ignored, it releases red/orange enemies, and later Splitters or Siphons. Killing it before a launch awards a small bonus. Usually only one is active.

### Siphon

Color: acid green.
Phase: 5+.
HP: 3.
Role: energy-drain shooter.

Fires green drain shots that remove energy rather than HP. If the player has no energy, drain shots do not become heavy HP damage.

### Leech

Color: dark green with green tether.
Phase: 6+.
HP: 4.
Role: proximity energy drain.

Locks on, then tethers to the player if close enough. The tether drains energy over time and breaks when the player gets far enough away or uses ghost movement well. It does not deal direct HP collision damage.

### Minecaster

Color: black hull with red/orange hazard lights.
Phase: 4+.
HP: 3.
Role: area denial.

Drops arming mines. Mines blink before activation, can be shot, and explode in a small radius if touched.

### Shieldbearer

Color: steel gray with yellow shield glow.
Phase: 5+.
HP: 4.
Role: support.

Applies a non-stacking shield to nearby enemies. The shield absorbs a hit on a cooldown. Shieldbearer itself is always vulnerable and never shields bosses.

### Railgunner

Color: dark red/black with red laser accents.
Phase: 7+.
HP: 3.
Role: telegraphed precision threat.

Slows before firing, paints a warning line for roughly 35-45 frames, locks direction, then fires a narrow beam. The beam does not track after lock.

### Repair Drone

Color: dull silver with green sparks.
Phase: 6+.
HP: 2.
Role: healer support.

Does not shoot. Repairs one damaged enemy slowly. Cannot repair bosses or asteroids. Rare and usually paired with Purple, Carrier, or Shieldbearer pressure.

## 9. Rare Environmental Hazard

### Debris Field

Event message: `DEBRIS FIELD`.
Phase: 3+.
Role: rare movement hazard.

Asteroids are not normal enemies. They are rare environmental events and boss tools.

Rules:

- Show warning text before the field appears.
- Do not trigger during normal boss fights.
- Do not trigger when the player is at 1 HP.
- Do not trigger when pressure is already high.
- Asteroids damage player and enemies.
- Asteroids block player shots.
- Asteroids should not become a normal powerup source.

Asteroid types:

- Small Debris: fast, low HP.
- Rock Asteroid: medium speed, medium HP.
- Iron Asteroid: slow, tanky, rare.
- Comet Shard: fast late-game asteroid with a trail.

## 10. Boss Roster

### Standard Boss

Color: cyan/white.
Role: baseline bullet and add-control boss.

Uses spread, aimed, fan, and spawn attacks.

### Wraith Boss

Color: pale ghost/realm tones.
Role: realm-system boss.

Requires physical/ghost realm awareness. The player must use ghost action to survive and to damage the Wraith in the correct state.

### Debris Warden

Color: dark metal, bronze, asteroid gray.
Role: asteroid-wall boss.
Core skill: move to the safe gap.

Summons asteroid walls with visible gaps. Patterns include single wall, double gate, crush wall, meteor warnings, and diagonal rotating fields. Heavy bullet spam must not overlap wall reads.

### Mothership

Color: bronze/gold.
Role: carrier boss.
Core skill: target priority and add control.

Launch bays release escorts, heavy deployments, and repair drones. Shooting the boss remains useful, but ignoring adds should make the fight harder.

### Siphon Core

Color: black-green and acid green.
Role: energy-drain boss.
Core skill: energy management.

Uses drain beams, energy mines, pulse rings, short-range tethers, and stronger drain patterns when the player has high energy. Empty energy should reduce drain pressure rather than become instant death.

### Hive Breaker

Color: yellow-green and amber fracture lines.
Role: splitter boss.
Core skill: handle break-apart chaos.

Releases shards, Splitter guards, and panic fragments as HP falls. It should reduce bullet pressure while fragments are active.

### Rail Tyrant

Color: black/dark red with laser core.
Role: laser boss.
Core skill: read telegraphs.

Uses center lance, crosshair lock, triple lanes, side sweep, and faster final warnings. No instant lasers. Beam direction locks before firing.

### Gravity Well

Color: dark violet/black with purple-red rings.
Role: movement-control boss.
Core skill: fight gentle movement pressure.

Creates warning circles, pull zones, compression rings, orbit shots, asteroid orbit releases, and pull-gap dodges. Pull must be noticeable but never fully steal control.

## 11. Powerups

Existing powerups:

- Spread Shot: temporary three-shot firing.
- Rapid Fire: temporary faster firing.
- Repair +1: restores 1 HP.
- Wingman: adds one escort.
- Dual Wing: adds two escorts.

Final added powerups:

- Energy Cell: restores 40-50 energy without exceeding max.
- Overcharge: increases energy regeneration for 10-15 seconds.
- Phase Shield: blocks one HP hit, does not block energy drain.
- Magnet Field: pulls powerups toward the player for about 12 seconds.
- Piercing Shot: center shots pierce one enemy for a short duration.
- Ion Burst: clears nearby enemy bullets and damages small enemies; little/no boss value.
- Stabilizer: reduces energy drain and weakens Leech/Gravity effects.
- Score Surge: temporary score multiplier boost, best during normal waves.

Powerups should support survival, energy play, offense, collection, and scoring without removing the need to dodge.

## 12. Fairness And Readability Rules

Readable action is more important than raw density.

Hard rules:

- Rail beams and boss lasers always telegraph.
- Beam direction locks before firing.
- Asteroid walls always show a visible safe gap.
- Leech and Siphon pressure energy, not HP.
- Gravity pull is gentle and never takes full control away.
- Bosses should not combine their identity mechanic with heavy unrelated spam.
- Support enemies create target priority but not permanent invulnerability.
- Shards are fragile and capped.
- Carriers are rare and priority focused.
- Debris fields are rare and pressure-aware.

Death should feel fair in retrospect.

## 13. Current Scope Guard

The final expansion package is now:

- 8 enemies: Splitter, Carrier, Siphon, Leech, Minecaster, Shieldbearer, Railgunner, Repair Drone.
- 1 environmental system: rare Debris Field / Asteroids.
- 6 bosses: Debris Warden, Mothership, Siphon Core, Hive Breaker, Rail Tyrant, Gravity Well.
- 8 powerups: Energy Cell, Overcharge, Phase Shield, Magnet Field, Piercing Shot, Ion Burst, Stabilizer, Score Surge.

Do not expand the roster further right now. Next work should focus on balance, polish, effects, achievement tuning, mobile feel, boss art readability, and performance.
